# Nour

## Healing Has Curves. Follow trends, not moments.

Nour is a local-first mood and wellbeing tracking application focused on privacy, emotional nuance, and long-term trend awareness rather than daily judgment or engagement-driven behavior.

The project was created in response to common frustrations with modern mental health and wellness products, many of which feel overly clinical, heavily gamified, subscription-driven, or optimized primarily for retention metrics rather than user reflection and trust.

Nour explores how emotionally sensitive products can be designed to feel calm, observational, and human while still maintaining clarity, usability, and long-term engagement.

---

## Product Overview

Nour is designed around a simple core premise:

> Individual emotional states fluctuate constantly, but long-term patterns are often more meaningful than isolated good or bad days.

The product intentionally emphasizes:

- Trend visualization over score-chasing
- Reflection over optimization
- Emotional neutrality over judgment
- User agency over engagement pressure

The current prototype focuses on validating whether low-pressure emotional trend tracking can provide meaningful long-term value without relying on addictive behavioral mechanics common in wellness applications.

---

## Core Product Principles

Nour is built around several guiding product and UX principles:

- Privacy should be a default expectation, not a premium feature
- Emotional states should not be moralized or treated as performance metrics
- Long-term patterns matter more than isolated entries
- Users define their own emotional baseline
- Mental health tools should minimize emotional pressure and cognitive overload
- Reflection tools should support users without attempting to replace professional care

### The product intentionally avoids:

- AI-generated diagnoses
- Automated emotional interpretation
- “Wellness streak” systems
- Manipulative retention mechanics
- Surveillance-based product models
- Engagement loops designed around guilt or obligation

## Current Features

### Daily Check-Ins

Users record lightweight daily emotional and behavioral reflections through a simplified mobile-first flow.

Current tracked categories include:

- Mood
- Energy
- Sleep

Additional categories are intentionally planned as optional future toggles rather than default requirements in order to reduce onboarding friction and avoid overwhelming users during early adoption.

---

### Trend-Based Visualization

Rather than emphasizing individual daily scores, Nour focuses on helping users observe emotional patterns over time through:

- Moving averages
- Long-term trend visualization
- Composite emotional tracking
- Visually softened historical review

The product direction intentionally prioritizes reducing emotional overreaction to isolated difficult days while encouraging longer-term self-awareness.

---

### Local-First Architecture

All user data is currently stored locally on-device.

The prototype intentionally avoids mandatory accounts, cloud infrastructure, or external data collection during early development in order to:

- Reinforce user trust
- Reduce privacy concerns
- Simplify onboarding
- Maintain product clarity during validation

Future sync and export systems are planned to remain explicitly opt-in.

---

### Therapist-Friendly Export

Nour includes printable export functionality designed to support optional discussion with therapists or trusted support systems.

Exports focus on:

- Visual trend summaries
- Optional written reflections
- Emotionally neutral presentation

The product intentionally avoids generating diagnostic conclusions or AI-driven emotional interpretation.

---

## Product & UX Decisions

A major focus of the project has been exploring how emotionally sensitive software changes when engagement optimization is no longer treated as the primary business objective.

| Decision | Product Reasoning |
|---|---|
| No streak systems | Avoid guilt-based engagement pressure |
| Local-first storage | Reinforce trust and reduce onboarding friction |
| Minimal onboarding | Lower cognitive load and reduce abandonment |
| Trend-focused graphing | Encourage long-term reflection instead of day-to-day emotional volatility |
| Optional category expansion | Preserve simplicity while allowing future personalization |
| No AI emotional analysis | Avoid overmedicalization and false authority |

The project intentionally prioritizes emotional safety, trust, and sustainable interaction design over maximizing short-term engagement metrics.
